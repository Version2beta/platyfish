This site is a teaching resource.

Plant a Fish's first project allowed us to work with Urban Assembly New York Harbor School, a public shool in Bushwick, Brooklyn. That was 2010, and our commitment to education and youth outreach continues to be a primary focus.

Today we work with schools and homeschoolers in multiple countries, and provide teaching materials and projects for thousands of students.

Some of the teaching resources we offer

Classroom materials. Each of our projects offers a downloadable packet of fact sheets, science unit curriculum, and motivational materials.

Fundraising projects for students. Our fundraising system allows teachers, students, and individuals to create their own personal fundraising campaigns underneath of any of our projects. Personal projects have their own goals, social media integration, favorites, and contributors. Students can participate in classroom fundraising projects or even set up their own.

Plant a Fish in the classroom. As often as possible, Plant a Fish sends hands-on volunteers and representatives into classrooms to share their experience and passion with students. Fabien Cousteau is particularly fond of the time he is able to spend sharing his love of the ocean.

A classroom in the field. Plant a Fish is working in dozens of locations throughout the western hemisphere. Students of all ages can volunteer to work with experienced oceanographers, biologists, and volunteers.
