Sea Turtles at Isla Cedros

This is part of the Sea Turtles campaign.

Location Isla Cedros, Baja California, Mexico
Goal 10,000 sea turtles ($25,000)
Deadline 30 March 2014

The eastern Pacific hawksbill turtles are the most endangered population of sea turtles in the world. Hope was lost to save them until very recently, a small but viable population was found nesting in the mangroves of El Salvador. Working with the skills of the tortugeros (turtle egg hunters) and others, Plant A Fish will re-plant one billion sea turtles to ensure the restoration of this species and others.

Through Plant A Fish and its special Salvadorian-based ViVAZUL initiative, Fabien is committed to restoring endangered sea turtles—and empowering others to do the same. Now, Fabien makes frequent trips to El Salvador, where he works with partners like ViVAZUL to engage local communities in helping “replant” baby sea turtles.
